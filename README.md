# Moremi Digital Skills

A public library of Claude skills for founders and senior leaders. Each skill is a structured instruction set that loads into Claude to improve performance on a specific, high-value task.

These skills are built to a single standard: every skill in this library should be something you would pay for if someone else had built it first.

---

## What is a Claude skill?

A skill is a file you upload to Claude that gives it a precise set of instructions for a specific task. Rather than prompting Claude from scratch each time, a skill loads a tested framework directly into the conversation, so Claude applies it consistently and completely without you having to specify how.

Skills work in Claude.ai on any plan. You do not need technical knowledge to use them.

---

## How to use a skill

1. Open [Claude.ai](https://claude.ai)
2. Start a new conversation
3. Click the paperclip or attachment icon
4. Upload the `SKILL.md` file from the skill folder you want to use
5. Tell Claude what you need. The skill will guide its approach automatically.

You can upload more than one skill file into the same conversation if your task spans multiple areas.

## Skills in this library

### [Authentic Writing Voice](./authentic-writing-voice/)
A prohibitions-based skill that removes AI writing patterns from any content before it is published. Covers structural patterns, sentence-level constructions, punctuation, and register. Applies to LinkedIn posts, articles, proposals, email copy, and any prose where voice and authenticity matter. Use it for generating new content or auditing existing drafts.

### [Board Reporting Brief](./board-reporting-brief/)
A structured preparation process for board meetings that covers what decisions need to be made versus reported, the likely concerns of each board member, agenda sequencing, explicit asks, and disagreement preparation. The output is a brief for the founder's own use in the forty-eight hours before the meeting.

### [Decision Audit](./decision-audit/)
A structured audit framework for examining the quality of reasoning behind a past decision, independent of how it turned out. Works through decision capture, assumption mapping, information audit, reasoning quality, bias assessment, risk assessment, outcome separation, pattern identification, reconstruction, and framing for others. Domain-agnostic. Applies to strategic, operational, financial, hiring, and product decisions.

### [Exit Readiness Audit](./exit-readiness-audit/)
A structured audit of how prepared a business is for a potential exit, across five dimensions: financial hygiene, legal and IP cleanliness, customer and revenue concentration, operational dependency on key individuals, and strategic narrative coherence. Issues are prioritised by transaction impact and remediation effort. Designed to be run annually regardless of whether an exit is being actively considered.

### [Founder Market Fit Review](./founder-market-fit-review/)
A structured assessment of whether a founder's specific capabilities, risk profile, decision-making style, personal economics, and definition of success are genuinely well-matched to what their chosen market requires in order to win. Assesses fit across six dimensions and classifies each as strong, workable, or constrained. Most valuable run annually.

### [Market Signal Review](./market-signal-review/)
A structured assessment that determines whether a specific piece of market feedback, customer input, competitive movement, or data point warrants a strategic response, a monitoring brief, or active discounting. Covers source assessment, corroboration mapping, bias assessment, and decision implication analysis.

### [Organisation Debt Audit](./organisation-debt-audit/)
A structured audit that maps the structural and operational liabilities a growing organisation has accumulated across five categories: structural, role, process, decision, and cultural debt. Prioritises each item by growth impact and remediation effort and produces specific recommendations for the highest-priority issues.

### [Pivot Decision Review](./pivot-decision-review/)
A structured assessment that determines whether the evidence supports a strategic pivot, what category of pivot it supports, and what the minimum viable test of the new direction is before full commitment. Resolves the execution versus strategy question first, then classifies the pivot type if strategy is the problem.

### [Strategic Assumption Review](./strategic-assumption-review/)
A structured pre-commitment review that surfaces every assumption embedded in a strategic initiative before resources are committed. Classifies each assumption by how load-bearing it is and how testable it is, designs minimum viable tests for the highest-risk items, and names the irreducible bets the founder is taking consciously rather than discovering mid-execution.

## Using skills together

Individual skills address specific problems. Some situations benefit from using two or three skills in sequence. The combinations below reflect the most common high-stakes scenarios founders face.

**Preparing for a fundraise**
Run the Strategic Assumption Review on your fundraise thesis before you start conversations. Use the Decision Audit if a prior strategic decision is likely to come up in investor diligence and you want to be able to speak to it clearly. Use the Board Reporting Brief to prepare for pitch meetings where investors will be probing your reasoning rather than just reviewing your deck.

**Navigating a strategic inflection point**
If you are questioning whether the business needs to change direction, start with the Pivot Decision Review to resolve whether the problem is execution or strategy. If the review identifies a strategic problem, follow with the Strategic Assumption Review on the proposed new direction before committing. If the inflection point is revealing structural issues in the organisation, run the Organisation Debt Audit in parallel.

**Preparing for a board meeting**
Use the Board Reporting Brief to prepare the conversation, not just the content. If the meeting involves a significant strategic decision, use the Strategic Assumption Review on that decision first so you arrive with the assumption stack mapped and the Toulmin structure prepared for each decision item.

**Annual business review**
Run the Founder Market Fit Review to assess whether your fit with the market has evolved as the business has grown. Run the Exit Readiness Audit to identify issues that will be costly to address close to a transaction. Run the Organisation Debt Audit to surface structural liabilities before they compound further.

**Evaluating a major market signal**
Use the Market Signal Review to determine whether the signal warrants a strategic response. If it does, and the response involves a significant new initiative, follow with the Strategic Assumption Review before committing resources.
